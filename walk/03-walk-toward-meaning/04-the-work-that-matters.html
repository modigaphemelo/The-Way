The Work That Matters

Practice 4 of 6: Distinguishing cathedrals from sandcastles

Not All Work Is Equal

You can work your entire life and build nothing that lasts. Or you can work your entire life and build something that outlasts you. The difference is not effort — it's architecture. Not intensity — it's intention. Not hours — it's direction.

Some work is sandcastles — beautiful in the moment, washed away by the next tide. Some work is cathedrals — built to last generations, designed to be added to, intended to outlive the builder. The question is not whether your work is good. It's whether your work matters.

Sandcastles vs Cathedrals

Sandcastle Work

  • Built for the moment
  • Washes away with next tide
  • No one builds upon it
  • Requires constant rebuilding
  • Impressive but temporary
  • Example: Content that's only relevant today, work that serves only yourself, projects that end when you stop

Cathedral Work

  • Built for generations
  • Designed to weather storms
  • Others can add to it
  • Requires maintenance, not rebuilding
  • Grows more valuable with time
  • Example: Systems that outlast you, knowledge that helps others, institutions that serve generations

The Question

Most of your work is probably sandcastles. That's fine — sandcastles have their place. But if all your work is sandcastles, you're building nothing that lasts. What cathedral are you building?

Four Qualities of Work That Matters

It Outlasts You

Designed to continue without you. A system that runs itself. Knowledge that teaches itself. An institution with its own life. Work that doesn't require your constant presence to keep working.

Others Can Build On It

It's a platform, not just a product. A foundation others can use. Ideas others can extend. Tools others can improve. Work that invites contribution.

It Compounds

Its value grows over time. Each year it becomes more valuable, not less. Knowledge that builds on itself. Relationships that deepen. Systems that improve with use.

It Would Be Missed

If it disappeared, people would notice. Not because of your name, but because of its absence. It serves a need. It fills a gap. It matters to someone other than you.

The Cathedral Exercise

Spend 30 minutes with these questions. Write freely.

1. The Longevity Question: What have you built that could outlast you? What could you build that would?

2. The Platform Question: What have you created that others can build upon? What could become a foundation?

3. The Compounding Question: What in your life becomes more valuable with time? What could you invest in that would compound?

4. The Gap Question: What problem do you see that no one else is solving? What need is going unmet? What could you build that would fill that gap?

5. The Legacy Question: If you could build one thing that would matter to people you'll never meet, what would it be?

Your One Cathedral

⛪

You don't need many. You need one.

You can build sandcastles every day and still build a cathedral. The cathedral is the work that matters — the project, the system, the body of work that outlasts you. Everything else is just practice.

What is your cathedral? Not what you're working on this week. Not what's urgent. What is the one thing that, if you built it, would make everything else worthwhile?

You might not know yet. That's fine. But start asking the question. Start looking for clues. Your cathedral is waiting to be built.

This Week's Practice

Day 1-2: Complete the Cathedral Exercise

Write your answers to all five questions. Don't edit. Just explore.

Day 3: Identify Your Cathedral

Based on your answers, what's your best guess at your cathedral? Write it down. It's okay if it's vague.

Day 4-6: One Cathedral Step

Take one small step toward your cathedral. Not a huge step — a small one. Research. Plan. Start something tiny.

Day 7: Reflect

How did it feel? What did you learn? What's next?

You don't build a cathedral in a day. But you lay the first stone today.
